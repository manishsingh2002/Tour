Day-by-Day Plan

The Complete 10-Day Journey

Depart Surat on Tuesday, arrive Rameswaram Thursday evening. Explore for two full days. Then sweep north through Kanyakumari, Madurai, Trichy, and Thanjavur.

✦
Day1–2
Surat → On Train → South India
The Long Ride South
Board Train 16734 at Udhna (Surat) at 21:20 on Tuesday. Keep your window seat — this is one of India's most scenic rail routes passing the Konkan coast and Kerala backwaters.
🚆
Tuesday 21:20
Board at Udhna (Surat)

Arrive at the Sacred Island
Train arrives at 19:15. Check into your hotel and make your way to Agni Teertham for the evening sea bath.

20:30 PM — Evening
Agni Teertham — Sacred Sea Shore
The sacred stretch of sea just outside the Ramanathaswamy Temple. Beautiful in the evening with temple bells echoing.
Evening visit for atmosphere. Come back early morning for the actual ritual bath.

Day4
Rameswaram Island
Temple Day — The Heart
Start at 5:30 AM for the 22-theertham bath ritual, then explore the island's spiritual landmarks.
🛕
5:30 AM
Ramanathaswamy Temple — Jyotirlinga
One of India's 12 Jyotirlingas. Walk the world's longest temple corridor and take the 22 holy theertham baths inside. Expect to get completely drenched.
Carry 2 changes of clothes. No camera/phone inside. Hire a guide (₹200–300).
⭐ 4.73 hrs
🙏
9:30 AM
Panchmukhi Hanuman & Floating Stones
Houses the legendary floating stones (Ram Setu stones) — believed used by Vanarasena to build the bridge to Lanka.
⛰️
12:00 PM
Gandhamadhana Parvatham
The highest point on Rameswaram island with Rama's sacred footprints. 360° panoramic views of the island.
🌉
5:00 PM
Pamban Bridge Sunset
India's iconic sea bridge. Time your visit to watch a train cross at sunset.
Day5
Dhanushkodi & Beaches
Land's End
Head to the ghost town ruins where the Bay of Bengal meets the Indian Ocean. Sri Lanka is visible 23 km away.
🏖️
8:30 AM
Dhanushkodi Beach & Point
The final point of land before Lanka. Two seas with visibly different colors meeting in a dramatic confluence.
Take a shared jeep (₹100). Arrive before 9 AM to beat the brutal sun.
🏊
2:00 PM
Ariyaman Beach
A calm, clean beach 15 km from town. Safe for swimming with clear turquoise water.
Day6
Kanyakumari (5.5 hrs by bus)
Where Three Seas Meet
Travel to the southernmost tip of mainland India.
🏛️
12:30 PM
Vivekananda Rock Memorial
Reached by ferry. Marks where Swami Vivekananda meditated. Includes the 133-foot Thiruvalluvar Statue.
🌇
5:30 PM
Sunset at Triveni Sangam
Watch the sun descend directly into the sea at the convergence of three oceans.
On full moon nights, you can see the sunset and moonrise simultaneously.
Day7
Madurai (4 hrs by bus)
The Temple City
One of the oldest continually inhabited cities in the world.
🛕
10:00 AM
Meenakshi Amman Temple
14 soaring gopurams and the Hall of 1,000 Pillars. The temples within temples are genuinely labyrinthine.
The evening aarti (around 9 PM) is unforgettable. No phones allowed.
🏛️
1:30 PM
Thirumalai Nayakkar Palace
17th-century royal palace featuring massive pillars and Indo-Saracenic architecture.
Day8
Trichy & Thanjavur
The Chola Legacy
World-record temples and ancient rockforts.
🛕
8:30 AM
Sri Ranganathaswamy Temple
The largest functioning Hindu temple complex in the world (156 acres). It feels like walking through an ancient walled city.
🏛️
2:00 PM (Thanjavur)
Brihadeeswarar Temple (UNESCO)
Built 1,000 years ago entirely of granite without mortar. The vimana casts no shadow at noon.
🚆
Evening
Board Return Train (via Chennai)

Temple Rules
  • Men MUST wear Dhotis inside major temples (rent outside for ₹50).
  • No phones/cameras allowed inside sanctums.
  • Arrive before 6 AM to beat extreme crowds.
Logistics
  • Carry ₹3,000 cash. Auto rickshaws and small towns don't use UPI.
  • TNSTC local buses are the fastest, cheapest way between cities.
  • Drink ORS daily. The humidity and heat drain energy quickly.
